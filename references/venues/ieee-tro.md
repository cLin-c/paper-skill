# IEEE Transactions on Robotics (T-RO) submission profile

Last checked: **2026-07-20**. Re-open the official pages before every submission; do not rely on this snapshot when the live page differs.

Primary sources:

- T-RO Information for Authors: https://www.ieee-ras.org/publications/t-ro/t-ro-information-for-authors/
- T-RO submission procedures: https://www.ieee-ras.org/publications/t-ro/submission-procedures/
- IEEE submission and peer-review policies, including AI disclosure: https://journals.ieeeauthorcenter.ieee.org/become-an-ieee-journal-author/publishing-ethics/guidelines-and-policies/submission-and-peer-review-policies/

## Initial-submission checks

1. Confirm article type: regular paper, communication item, or survey paper. Survey papers require an Editor-in-Chief proposal before manuscript submission.
2. Confirm the live page limit. At the access date, initial regular papers are limited to 18 Transactions-format pages; communication items to 4; survey papers to 20. Limits include figures, tables, and references.
3. Use IEEE Transactions double-column format. At the access date, regular and survey LaTeX manuscripts use `\documentclass[journal]{IEEEtran}` at 10 pt; communication items use `\documentclass[9pt,technote]{IEEEtran}`.
4. Apply double-anonymous review requirements. T-RO states that author names must not appear in the initial PDF; audit acknowledgments, self-citations, supplements, filenames, PDF metadata, repositories, and links for identity leakage.
5. Check abstract limits on the live page. At the access date: 200 words for regular/survey papers and 50 words for communication items.
6. Confirm scope fit and substantive novelty relative to prior versions. A submission based on prior publications must be more than added detail, experiments, or narrative.
7. Confirm the manuscript is not simultaneously under review elsewhere.
8. Prepare the cover letter, including special-issue identification when applicable and only conflict-free reviewer suggestions/exclusions with reasons.
9. Audit AI use against the current IEEE policy. At the access date, AI-generated article content must be disclosed in acknowledgments with the system, affected sections, and level of use; editing/grammar use is generally outside the mandatory rule but disclosure is recommended.
10. Verify multimedia, preprint, copyright, open-access, and page-charge rules from the live official pages rather than memory.

## Required output

Produce a dated table with `requirement`, `manuscript evidence`, `status`, `official URL`, and `accessed_at`. Use `VERIFIED`, `UNVERIFIED`, and `AUTHOR_INPUT_NEEDED`. Never mark the package ready because it merely fits the page limit.
